Hello, I'm Jhonatan!

I am a foreign language learner on a journey to master English. This blog is a space where I share my experiences, challenges, and progress throughout this process. Learning a new language is not just about grammar and vocabulary — it is about discovering new ways to express yourself and connect with the world.

Over these weeks I have worked on five meaningful activities that pushed me to grow. Each one taught me something different and helped me become more confident using English in real situations.

What I've worked on

My Five Learning Activities

Here are the five activities I completed as part of my English learning process.

01
Reading & Comprehension

Activity 1

In this first activity I worked on developing my reading comprehension and vocabulary skills. It was a great starting point because it helped me understand how to identify key ideas in a text and how to connect them with my own knowledge. I learned that reading carefully — not just quickly — makes a huge difference in understanding.

View Activity
02
Writing & Expression

Activity 2

The second activity focused on writing and expressing my own ideas in English. This was challenging because putting your thoughts into words in a foreign language requires you to think twice about every sentence. However, it was incredibly rewarding to see my writing improve little by little. I learned to organize my ideas more clearly and use linking words to make my text flow better.

View Activity
03
Speaking & Pronunciation

Activity 3

Activity three pushed me outside my comfort zone — I had to speak. Pronunciation and fluency are areas I find particularly difficult, so practicing out loud was both nerve-wracking and necessary. Through this activity I discovered that the more you speak, the less scary it becomes. I gained confidence and learned that making mistakes is simply part of the process.

View Activity
04
Listening & Understanding

Activity 4

Listening comprehension was the focus of my fourth activity. Understanding spoken English, especially at natural speed, is one of the hardest parts of learning the language. This activity helped me train my ear to catch words and phrases I would normally miss. I also learned useful strategies for understanding meaning even when I do not know every single word.

View Activity
05
Grammar & Structure

Activity 5

My fifth activity brought everything together by focusing on grammar and sentence structure. Understanding the rules of the language helped me become more precise in both writing and speaking. I realized that grammar is not about memorizing rules — it is about understanding why the language works the way it does, and that understanding makes every other skill stronger.

View Activity
Looking back

My Final Reflection

This English learning process has been one of the most enriching experiences of my academic life. When I started, I felt overwhelmed by how much there was to learn — the vocabulary, the grammar, the pronunciation, the listening. But looking back, I can see how far I have come.

Each of the five activities I completed taught me something unique. Together, they gave me a much more complete picture of what it means to communicate in another language. It is not just about knowing words — it is about thinking, listening, and expressing yourself in a whole new way.

I still have a long road ahead, but I am no longer afraid of it. Every challenge I faced during these activities made me more confident and more curious. English is no longer just a subject — it is becoming a part of who I am.
